Aviation Industry Insights

Why Aviation Is a High-Growth Industry

India is one of the fastest-growing aviation markets globally, driven by rising passenger demand, airport modernization, UDAN connectivity, and airline fleet expansion.

Key Challenges Facing Airlines

Airlines face challenges such as high fuel costs, pilot shortages, aircraft leasing expenses, regulatory compliance, and operational disruptions affecting profitability.

Aviation Laws & Passenger Rights

DGCA – India

The Directorate General of Civil Aviation regulates civil aviation safety, pilot licensing, aircraft certification, and passenger rights in India.

Passenger Rights in India

DGCA mandates compensation for flight delays, cancellations, denied boarding, and baggage loss. Airlines must offer refunds or alternate flights under prescribed timelines.

ICAO & IATA

ICAO sets global aviation standards for safety and security, while IATA represents airlines worldwide, focusing on operational efficiency and standardization.

Aviation Careers & Jobs

Cabin Crew

Roles include passenger safety, service excellence, and emergency handling. Requires DGCA compliance and airline-specific training.

Airport Operations

Involves terminal management, ground handling, safety coordination, and airline liaison roles.

Aviation Management

Careers in airline planning, revenue management, safety management systems (SMS), and regulatory affairs.
